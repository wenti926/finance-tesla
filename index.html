Tesla has six factories around the world:

  • The Tesla Factory is an automobile manufacturing plant in Fremont, California, operated by Tesla, Inc.
  • Tesla Giga Nevada (or Gigafactory 1) is a lithium-ion battery and electric vehicle subassembly factory near Reno, Nevada.
  • Tesla Giga New York (or Gigafactory 2) is a photovoltaic (PV) cell factory leased by Tesla subsidiary SolarCity in Buffalo, New York.
  • Tesla Giga Shanghai (Chinese: 特斯拉上海超级工厂, or Gigafactory 3) is a factory in Shanghai, China operated by Tesla, Inc.
  • Tesla Gigafactory Berlin-Brandenburg (also known as Gigafactory Berlin, Gigafactory 4 or Gigafactory Europe) is a European manufacturing plant for Tesla, Inc.
  • Tesla, Inc. has several factory buildings in the industrial zone of Vossenberg, Tilburg, in the Netherlands.
Stock is up an incredible 659% in 2020, making it one of most valuable companies in the world with a $630 billion valuation. Tesla stock is up more than 4000% since its debut 10 years age. The company raised around $226 million in its IPO, with shares surging that day by around 41% to close at $23.89, meaning Tesla’s stock has risen by 4,125% since the close of its first days as a public company.

But it's important to note that the generated gorss profit will after be used to pay operating expenses (research and development, administrative, etc) before getting the net income (before taxes)

Tesla is planning to grow thier manufacturing capacity as quickly as possible. Over a multi-year horizon, they expect to achieve 50% average annual growth in vehicle deliveries. In some years we may grow faster, which they expect to be the case in 2021. The rate of growth will depend on thier equipment capacity, operational efficiency and capacity and stability of the supply chain.
Total revenue grew 46% YoY in Q4. This was primarily achieved through substantial growth in vehicle deliveries as well as growth in other parts of the business. At the same time, vehicle average selling price (ASP) declined by 11% YoY as Teslas's product mix continued to shift from Model S and Model X to the more affordable Model 3 and Model Y.
Quarter-end cash and cash equivalents increased to $19.4B in Q4, driven mainly by Tesla's recent capital raise of $5.0B (average price of this offering was ~$632/share) and free cash flow of $1.9B, partially offset by early debt repayments (early conversion of convertible notes).
GAAP gross profit of $4.1B remained essentially flat in 2019 compared to 2018. Volume growth and successful cost reduction efforts were offset by normalization of ASP, mix shift towards Model 3 and a higher lease mix.
Sequentially, GAAP gross margin remained relatively flat in Q4 compared to Q3, while we ramped Model 3 production at Gigafactory Shanghai.
While we saw an increase in operating expenses in Q4 (driven mostly by $72M of non-cash SBC expense related to one more 2018 CEO award operational milestone becoming probable), higher gross profit resulted in a 72bp sequential improvement of GAAP operating margin to 4.9% in Q4.
On one hand we can notice that the operating margin and the adjusted ebitda have the same behavior. Both of them have started growing since 2017 and never stopped. On the other hand, the automative and total GAAP are moving together and tend to be almost constant
